Leveling Kits That Make Everyday Driving Feel Just Right

What Are Leveling Kits, Anyway?

A leveling kit raises the front of your truck or SUV to match the height of the rear. From the factory, most trucks come with a forward “rake”—that’s just a fancy way of saying the front sits a bit lower than the back. Manufacturers do it to help when you’re hauling heavy loads, but for everyday drivers, it often looks and feels uneven.

With a leveling kit, we give your truck the balance it deserves while improving clearance, making space for bigger tires, and giving it a sharper, more aggressive look.

Why They’re Worth It

We’ve installed them on all kinds of vehicles, including Ford, Chevy, Ram, Toyota, and more. Here’s why our customers keep asking for them:

1. A Clean, Balanced Stance

You don’t need to lift the whole truck to get that level, aggressive look. A leveling kit makes a subtle but powerful difference. Most of our customers love the change right away, especially when they see their truck looking right from every angle.

2. More Room for Tires

If you’re thinking about upgrading to bigger tires, a leveling kit can make the space you need. That extra inch or two up front can be the difference between a rub and a smooth fit.

3. Better Front-End Clearance

With that front-end rake gone, you get more ground clearance for dips, driveways, curbs, and off-road terrain. No more scraping the bumper or skid plate on minor obstacles.

4. Keeps the Ride Comfortable

Done right, a leveling kit keeps your factory ride intact. No weird bumps. No stiff suspension. Just a better feel behind the wheel.

What to Know About the Different Types

Not all kits are the same, and we’ll help you pick the one that works best for your suspension type and goals.

Strut Spacers

Perfect for most modern trucks and SUVs with independent front suspension. These are installed above the struts and give a simple lift without changing your ride quality.

Coil Spring Spacers

For trucks with solid front axles, like some heavy-duty models. We can add spacers or upgrade coils depending on how much lift you want.

Torsion Keys

For vehicles with torsion bar front ends (common in older GM trucks), we can adjust or replace the keys for a level front-end lift.

Every setup has its pros and cons—but don’t worry, we’ll walk you through it all before we touch a single bolt.

Is It Safe?
(Yes, If Done Right.)

We hear this question a lot, and honestly, it’s smart to ask. When done properly, a leveling kit is completely safe. But when installed the wrong way—or with low-quality parts—it can throw off your alignment, wear out your suspension, or create handling issues.

That’s why we do it the right way every time:

  • Careful inspection of all suspension components
  • Professional-grade installation
  • Precision alignment after the kit is installed
  • Final check to make sure everything handles the way it should

We treat your truck like our own. You can feel confident that your ride leaves our shop better than it came in.

How Much Lift Do You Actually Get?

In most cases, the front end gets a bump of about 1 to 2.5 inches, just enough to even things out, improve clearance, and fit larger tires without jumping into a full suspension overhaul.

And here’s our promise: we’ll never recommend more lift than your vehicle can safely handle. You’ll always get straightforward advice, real solutions, and a setup that actually works for the way you drive.

What’s the Right Call?

We get this question often, especially from drivers who are trying to plan future upgrades. Here’s the quick breakdown:

  • Go with a leveling kit if you want a cleaner stance, a little extra clearance, and a smoother ride without major changes.
  • Go with a full lift kit if you’re planning to add oversized tires, go off-road regularly, or want to raise the entire vehicle several inches.

Most of our daily drivers stick with this kind of upgrade, and they’re more than happy with the outcome. It’s a simple, effective way to fine-tune your ride without breaking the bank.
